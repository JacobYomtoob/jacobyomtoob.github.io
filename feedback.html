Personal Reflections

My Growth as an Educator

Reflection · 2024

On Starting Movement After Dark

When I founded the Movement After Dark sessions during residency, I wasn't sure how residents would respond to an additional educational commitment. What I discovered is that when learning is active, visual, and clearly tied to clinical relevance, engagement follows naturally. That experience shaped everything about how I design educational programs today.

Reflection · 2025

On Teaching Beyond the Resident

Expanding education to patients and care partners — through programs like the HD Bootcamp and Parkinson's support group presentations — has been one of the most meaningful developments in my educator identity. Teaching is not only a professional responsibility; it is a form of care.

Reflection · 2025

On Scholarship as a Teaching Tool

Every time I present research at a national conference or publish a paper, I am modeling for the residents I work with what it looks like to take a clinical question seriously, pursue it rigorously, and share the findings with the field. Research and teaching are not separate tracks — they reinforce each other.

Reflection · 2026

On the McGaw Scholars Program

Enrolling in the McGaw Health Professions Educator Clinical Scholars Program has given me a vocabulary and theoretical framework for things I had been doing intuitively. Understanding instructional design, assessment theory, and educational scholarship has made me a more intentional and effective educator.

Looking Forward

Areas for Continued Growth

Honest self-reflection is essential to growth as an educator. Below are areas I am actively working to develop.

→

Formal Assessment Design

I aim to build more robust pre- and post-assessments into my educational programs to rigorously measure learning outcomes beyond participant satisfaction.

→

Diverse Learner Contexts

Most of my teaching to date has been with residents and fellows. I am actively expanding into patient and community education, which requires a distinct set of instructional strategies I am still developing.

→

Technology in Medical Education

I am exploring how digital tools — from simulation to online modules — can extend the reach and impact of movement disorders education beyond institutional walls.
