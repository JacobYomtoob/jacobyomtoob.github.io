About

Jacob Yomtoob, MD

I am a board-certified neurologist and current Movement Disorders fellow at Northwestern University Feinberg School of Medicine. I completed my undergraduate studies in biochemistry and neuroscience at the University of Miami and attended medical school at Northwestern.

I completed my neurology residency at Mass General Brigham/Harvard Medical School, where I developed a strong interests in ataxia, Huntington's Disease, and movement disorders alongside a passion for medical education. During residency, I created Movement After Dark, a resident-driven quarterly movement disorders phenomenology video rounds program, in collaboration with movement disorders faculty at Mass General Brigham. I am currently completing fellowship training at Northwestern, with a clinical and academic focus on genetic movement disorders, including ataxia and Huntington's disease, and have continued my educational work by implementing movement phenomenology rounds for neurology residents as a fellow. My clinical work emphasizes patient-centered care, education, and multidisciplinary approaches to complex neurologic conditions. My recent scholarly work has focused on reviewing non-medication therapies for ataxia, developing a care partner education program for families affected by Huntington's disease, and resident-focused educational literature on rare genetic ataxias.

Following fellowship, I plan to join the Northwestern faculty, where my goal is to help further expand the multidisciplinary ataxia and Huntington's disease clinics in addition to providing evidence-based, patient centered Parkinson's Disease and Movement Disorders care. I am passionate about partnering with patients, families, and advocacy organizations to improve care, education, and access to emerging therapies for individuals living with Movement Disorders.
